This is 1932 Ford

In 1933 Ford revised their V8 model known as the Model 18 or Duece. The car received a longer wheelbase and an all-new front grill.
Many design traits were taken from the English Ford Model Y including the sloping front grill and hood louvers.


This is the Bugatti Type 35


Instantly recognizable from its tall silhouette and large diameter alloy wheels, it was a super star on the track throughout the 1920s and early ’30s, winning some 2,000 events in that time period.


This is 1963 Triumph TR4


The Triumph TR4 is a sports car produced by the Triumph Motor Company from 1961 to 1965. As the successor to the TR3A, the car was based on the chassis and drivetrain of the previous TR sports cars, but with a modern body designed by Michelotti.


This is Jaguar E-Type

Described as ‘The most beautiful car ever made’ by Enzo Ferrari, the Jaguar E‑type epitomised the glamour and excitement of the swinging 60s. Nearly 60 years on, the Jaguar E‑type Reborn programme will see Series 1 E‑types restored to their original specification for future generations of enthusiasts and collectors to buy directly from Jaguar Classic.
